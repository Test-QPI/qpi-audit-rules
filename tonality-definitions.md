# Tonality Definitions

This document defines how tone is classified in Admissions QPI auditing.
Classification is deterministic and must follow the rules below exactly.

---

## Evaluation Scope

- Tone is evaluated across the **entire call**
- Both agent language and delivery matter
- Student tone does not affect scoring

---

## Tonality Levels

### Level A — Exceptional
- Warm, confident, empathic
- Consistently supportive language
- Proactive reassurance without pressure

### Level B — Positive
- Professional and friendly
- Clear, respectful communication
- Minor hesitation allowed without impact

### Level C — Neutral
- Transactional
- Limited warmth or empathy
- No overtly negative language

### Level D — Negative
- Dismissive, impatient, or curt
- Defensive explanations
- Repeated interruptions

### Level E — Detrimental
- Aggressive or coercive
- Threatening consequences
- Explicit pressure or fear tactics

---

## Classification Rules

- Classify according to the **lowest sustained level**
- Do not average tone across moments
- If a lower level appears briefly but materially impacts the call, use the lower level

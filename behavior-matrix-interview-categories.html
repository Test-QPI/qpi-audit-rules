Global rules, evidence requirements, N/A rules, and opportunity selection rules

STAGE 1 – INTERVIEW

1) Rapport Development
Definition
Builds connection and trust while maintaining a professional, student-centered conversation.
Evidence indicators
- Uses student name naturally
- Reflects or validates student statements
- Establishes supportive tone without sounding scripted
Scoring anchors
5 = Two or more empathy/validation moments AND at least one personalized tie-back to student goals.
3 = At least one rapport behavior present, but inconsistent or surface-level.
1 = Transactional, scripted, or minimal connection effort.
Clarification — Empathy/Validation Moment Definition
An empathy/validation moment must explicitly acknowledge the student's feelings, goals,
or concerns using original phrasing. Generic affirmations ("awesome," "great," "perfect,"
"absolutely") do not qualify. Simple repetition of the student's words without validation
does not qualify. The language must demonstrate the agent received and responded to what
the student shared.

2) Controlled Discovery
Definition
Asks purposeful questions and follows up to understand goals, motivation, constraints, and fit.
Evidence indicators
- Open-ended questions about goals and motivations
- Follow-up probing (why, tell me more, what led you to)
- Summarizes or connects goals to program value

Scoring anchors
5 = Meets Path A or Path B.

Path A (question-driven):
- At least 2 open-ended discovery questions AND
- At least 1 follow-up probe AND
- At least 1 explicit tie-back connecting the student’s goal/motivation/barrier to program fit or the next step.

Path B (context volunteered by student):
- The student clearly states goals/motivation/barriers without being prompted AND
- The agent provides an explicit summary of what the student shared AND
- The agent provides at least 1 explicit tie-back connecting that information to program fit or the next step.

3 = Some discovery occurs, but Path A and Path B are not fully met (for example: mostly closed questions, no follow-up probe, or no tie-back).
1 = Little to no discovery; agent does not seek or use student goals/motivation/barriers.
Clarification — Score 1 Boundary
Score 1 ONLY if zero open-ended discovery questions occur AND zero goal/motivation/barrier
references appear anywhere in the call. If even one open-ended question or one reference
to student goals exists, score 3, not 1.

3) Financial Clarity (Interview)
Definition
Sets accurate expectations around tuition and the financial process without guarantees or avoidance.
Evidence indicators
- Addresses tuition or cost expectations accurately when relevant
- Introduces FAFSA/aid process accurately at a high level when relevant
- Avoids guarantees, minimization, or evasive responses
Scoring anchors
5 = Financial expectations explained clearly AND confirms student understanding (verbal check-in) AND no guarantee language.
3 = Financial topics mentioned but incomplete OR no confirmation of understanding.
1 = Avoids/deflects improperly, unclear tuition messaging, or any misleading framing.
Clarification — Confirmation of Understanding
Confirmation requires the student to explicitly affirm comprehension tied to a specific
concept — not generic acknowledgment. Examples that qualify: student restates what they
understood ("so it covers X"), student asks a follow-up question demonstrating engagement,
student explicitly confirms ("yes, that makes sense" tied to a specific point). A standalone
"okay," "got it," or "yep" without connection to a specific concept does not constitute
confirmation.

4) Risk Prevention (Interview)
Definition
Prevents financial aid misunderstandings and compliance risk during initial financial discussion.
Evidence indicators
- Explicitly avoids guarantee language about aid amounts or eligibility
- Sets expectation that awards/eligibility are determined later
- Prepares student for at least one common financial aid blocker when relevant (access, timing, documentation)
Scoring anchors
5 = Sets determination expectations clearly AND avoids guarantees AND prepares for at least one common blocker.
3 = Accurate but surface-level; limited prevention (no blockers or weak expectation setting).
1 = Any guarantee language or minimizing financial aid process.
Clarification — Surface-Level Definition
Surface-level prevention means the agent avoids guarantee language but does not address
timing, documentation, or eligibility uncertainty. To score above 3, the agent must
explicitly address at least one of: (1) timing — when eligibility or award amounts are
determined, (2) documentation — what the student must provide for FAFSA or aid processing,
or (3) eligibility uncertainty — that outcomes vary based on individual circumstances.

5) Assumptive Leadership
Definition
Uses confident, forward-moving language that keeps the student progressing, including when the student expresses hesitation, wants to shorten the call, or attempts to disconnect before the workflow is complete.

Evidence indicators
- Uses “next step” language (we will, next, let’s do this now)
- Maintains conversational control and direction
- Limits unnecessary optional phrasing
- Redirects hesitation, time pressure, or disconnect attempts into a narrowed but forward-moving plan
- Uses confident deflection language that keeps the student engaged long enough to secure the next appropriate step

Scoring anchors
5 = Two or more assumptive/forward statements AND maintains control through transitions, including at least one effective redirect of hesitation, time pressure, or disconnect resistance when relevant.
3 = Some leadership language is present, but it is inconsistent, overly optional, or only partially effective in keeping the call moving forward.
1 = Passive tone; student drives the call; agent repeatedly defers without attempting to progress, OR the agent allows hesitation/disconnect resistance to end forward movement without a meaningful redirect attempt.
Clarification — Redirect Requirement
The redirect requirement in the 5-anchor applies only when the transcript contains explicit
hesitation, time pressure, or disconnect cues from the student. If no such cues appear,
the 5-anchor redirect element is satisfied by default — do not penalize the agent for
absence of a situation that did not occur. Score based on forward-moving assumptive
language alone in those cases.

6) Progression Control
Definition
Secures the next required step with a clear action and timeline.

Evidence indicators
- Explicit next step identified (apply, FAFSA, docs, follow-up)
- Time-bound commitment or scheduled plan
- Confirms what will happen next
- Student agreement or acknowledgement is explicit when required

Scoring anchors
5 = Clear next step + TIME-BOUND commitment + student agreement.
    - TIME-BOUND means a specific date/time or deadline (today/by tomorrow/at 3pm), OR the next step is completed in-call.
    - Student agreement must be explicit (yes/okay/that works/I’ll do that).

3 = Clear next step stated, but the timeline is non-time-bound (e.g., “when you can,” “soon,” “email me when done”) OR student agreement is not explicit.

1 = No clear next step, no timeline, or call ends without a progression plan.

Clarification
- Assumptive Leadership and objection handling may help keep the call moving, but they do NOT by themselves satisfy Progression Control.
- To score Progression Control, the call must still result in a clearly identified next step and a secured plan at the standard required by the anchors above.
- A successful redirect of hesitation, time pressure, or disconnect resistance without a secured next-step plan should support Assumptive Leadership, not Progression Control.

STAGE 2 – APPLICATION

7) Technical Guidance Precision (Application)
Definition
Gives clear, step-by-step application guidance and resolves obstacles.
Evidence indicators
- Walks through steps in order (where to go, what to click/enter)
- Troubleshoots barriers
- Confirms what the student will do next
Scoring anchors
5 = Step-by-step guidance AND addresses a barrier OR confirms completion plan explicitly.
3 = General instructions given but lacks structure or confirmation.
1 = Vague, incorrect, or confusing guidance.
Clarification — Step-by-Step Definition
Step-by-step guidance requires at least two ordered, sequential actions directed to the
student (e.g., "go to the app store, download Full Sail One, then click the profile button").
A single directional statement or a general description of the process does not constitute
step-by-step guidance and should be scored 3.

8) Accountability Setting (Application)
Definition
Creates ownership and urgency to complete the application.

Evidence indicators
- Reinforces importance of completing
- Secures time-bound commitment
- Sets expectations for follow-up
- Confirms completion when completed in-call

Scoring anchors
5 = Application is completed and submitted during the call (proof-locked).
    - Must include explicit confirmation of submission/completion (agent or student/parent), e.g., "submitted", "hit submit", "confirmation page", "application completed."
    - If submission is blocked (verification code not received, portal error, FSO registration interruption), score cannot be 5.

3 = Time-bound deadline + student agreement + follow-up expectation, but application is NOT completed on-call.
    - Deadline must be specific (today/by tomorrow/at 3pm/within 1 hour).
    - Follow-up expectation must be explicit (agent states when/how they will reconnect OR what action triggers follow-up, e.g., "text/email me once complete").
    - Note: Time-bound commitment cannot score 5 unless submission is completed during the call.

1 = Passive encouragement only; no specific deadline, no explicit follow-up expectation, no ownership established.

STAGE 3 – FAFSA

9) Process Clarity (FAFSA)
Definition
Explains FAFSA steps and requirements clearly without overpromising.
Evidence indicators
- Directs to correct access path (studentaid.gov)
- Explains FSA ID at a basic level when relevant
- Clarifies what the student must do next
Scoring anchors
5 = Clear steps + next action plan + VERIFIED understanding.
    - VERIFIED understanding requires the student to clearly confirm the next action (e.g., “Yes, I’m going to submit it today,” or repeats what they will do next).
    - A simple “okay” without clarity does not count as verified understanding.
3 = Clear explanation is provided, but next action plan is vague OR understanding is not verified.
Clarification
- Giving the correct FAFSA year, link, or general next step does NOT by itself satisfy the 5-anchor.
- To score 5, the agent must provide clear steps, a clear next action plan, AND verified understanding at the standard defined above.
- A simple acknowledgment, generic “okay,” or broad agreement does NOT count as verified understanding.
1 = Incorrect, vague, or misleading explanation.

10) Risk Prevention (FAFSA)
Definition
Prepares student for common FAFSA issues and avoids compliance risk.
Evidence indicators
- Avoids guarantee language about awards
- Mentions common blockers (docs, dependency, timing) when relevant
- Sets expectation that eligibility/amount is determined later
Scoring anchors
5 = Explicitly sets determination expectation + avoids guarantees + prepares for at least one common blocker.
3 = Accurate but surface-level; limited prevention.
1 = Any guarantee language or minimizing FAFSA process.
Clarification — Surface-Level Definition
Surface-level prevention means the agent avoids guarantee language but does not address
timing, documentation, or eligibility uncertainty specific to FAFSA. To score above 3,
the agent must explicitly address at least one of: (1) timing — when FAFSA determines
eligibility or award amounts, (2) documentation — what the student must have ready
(tax info, FSA ID, dependency status), or (3) eligibility uncertainty — that aid amounts
vary and are not guaranteed.

11) Accountability Setting (FAFSA)
Definition
Secures a clear ownership plan for FAFSA completion and establishes expectations for what happens next.

Evidence indicators
- Establishes a timeline or immediate workflow expectation for FAFSA completion
- Secures student commitment or acknowledgement
- Clarifies follow-up expectations
- When applicable, executes the scripted FAFSA handoff appropriately by leaving the student at the FAFSA workflow handoff point and setting expectations for continued completion and follow-up

Scoring anchors
5 = ONE of the following is explicitly present:

A) TIME-BOUND completion path
- Specific deadline + student agreement + follow-up expectation tied to FAFSA completion.
- Deadline must be specific (today/by tomorrow/at 3pm) OR FAFSA is completed in-call.
- Follow-up expectation must be explicit (agent states when/how they will reconnect or what action triggers follow-up).

OR

B) Scripted FAFSA handoff path
- The agent actively progresses the student to the FAFSA workflow handoff point, AND
- The agent explicitly states that the student will continue the government form independently because the agent cannot remain on the phone for that portion, AND
- The agent secures a short follow-up expectation tied to FAFSA completion, AND
- The student explicitly agrees or acknowledges the plan.

Campus Mentor Handoff Clarification
- For campus students following the approved Campus Mentor handoff script, the 5-anchor scripted FAFSA handoff path may still be satisfied even if the original Admissions Representative does not personally schedule the short callback, provided the transcript explicitly shows:
  - Campus Mentor / campus-team handoff as the immediate next-step owner,
  - Campus Mentor framed as the main point of contact going forward,
  - and explicit instruction for the student to continue FAFSA in the meantime.
- This exception applies only when the campus handoff is clearly script-driven and immediate, not when the follow-up is generic, indefinite, or assigned to an unspecified team member.

3 = FAFSA ownership is established, but the completion path is incomplete at the 5-anchor level.
Examples:
- FAFSA completion is encouraged and the next step is clear, but the timeline is vague/non-time-bound, OR
- follow-up expectation is present but not short/specific enough for the scripted handoff 5-anchor, OR
- the student acknowledges the plan generally, but explicit agreement or timing is weaker than required for a 5.

1 = No meaningful ownership plan for FAFSA completion.
Examples:
- FAFSA is mentioned only as a future task,
- no deadline or immediate workflow expectation is established,
- no real follow-up expectation is set,
- or the agent leaves FAFSA as a general suggestion without securing student ownership of the next step.

Clarification
- A valid FAFSA handoff should not be scored as a 1 if the agent clearly advances the student into the FAFSA process and establishes what happens next, but falls short of the strict 5-anchor timing requirements.
- In those cases, score 3 unless the transcript clearly meets the 5-anchor path above.
- A specific callback time does NOT by itself make the FAFSA completion path time-bound.
To qualify for the 5-anchor time-bound path, the student’s own FAFSA completion expectation must also be specific and time-bound.

STAGE 4 – DOCUMENTS

12) Process Clarity (Documents)
Definition
Explains required documents and how to submit them clearly.
Evidence indicators
- Names at least one specific document
- Explains where/how to upload or submit
- Confirms student understanding
Scoring anchors
5 = Names specific document(s) + explains submission path + confirms understanding/next action.
3 = Mentions documents/portal but lacks specificity or confirmation.
1 = Vague instructions; student left unclear on what/where/how.

13) Accountability Setting (Documents)
Definition
Secures a time-bound plan for document submission.
Evidence indicators
- Sets timeline expectation
- Secures commitment
- Clarifies follow-up expectations
Scoring anchors
5 = Timeline + commitment + follow-up expectation.
3 = Encourages completion but timeline vague.
1 = No timeline or accountability.

LOWEST_BEHAVIOR_CATEGORY SELECTION RULES (DETERMINISTIC)
Use this section to determine:
- Lowest_Behavior_Category
- Lowest_Behavior_Score

OPPORTUNITY ELIGIBILITY GATE (HARD RULE)
- Opportunities may ONLY be selected from behavior categories scored 1 or 3.
- A category scored 5 is NEVER eligible to be an Opportunity.
- If no categories are scored 1 or 3, then Opportunity_1 = None, Opportunity_2 = None, Opportunity_3 = None.
- If a selected Opportunity does not have a score of 1 or 3, replace it with the next eligible category by priority order. If none exist, output None.

Step 1: Build eligible scored category list
- Include all applicable behavior categories that were scored for this call.
- Exclude unscored categories from stages that were not present.

Step 2: Determine Lowest_Behavior_Score
- Lowest_Behavior_Score is the lowest numeric score among eligible scored categories.
- Allowed values remain 1, 3, or 5.

Step 3: Determine Lowest_Behavior_Category
- Select the category or categories tied at Lowest_Behavior_Score.
- If only one category is tied, output that label.
- If multiple categories are tied, select the highest-priority tied category using the fixed priority order below.

Fixed Priority Order for tie-breaks
Progression Control
Assumptive Leadership
Accountability Setting (Application)
Accountability Setting (FAFSA)
Accountability Setting (Documents)
Controlled Discovery
Rapport Development
Financial Clarity (Interview)
Process Clarity (FAFSA)
Process Clarity (Documents)
Risk Prevention (Interview)
Risk Prevention (FAFSA)
Technical Guidance Precision (Application)

Step 4: Output rule
- Lowest_Behavior_Category must be exactly one label from this matrix.
- Do not output multiple labels.
- Do not output a custom label.

OPPORTUNITY SELECTION RULES (UNSCORED)
Always output up to three Opportunities using the labels above.
No custom opportunity names allowed.

Opportunity Selection Decision Tree (Deterministic)

Step 1: Build candidate list
- Include any behavior categories scored as 1 or 3 from applicable stages.
- Exclude categories scored 5.

Step 2: Mandatory inclusion rules

2A) Score-1 override (HARD)
If any applicable behavior category is scored 1, Opportunity_1 must equal Lowest_Behavior_Category.
This overrides Progression_Success-based forcing rules.

2B) Progression failure escalation
If 2A does NOT apply AND Progression_Success = NO AND Progression Control is scored 3 or lower,
then Opportunity_1 must be Progression Control.

2C) Lowest alignment rule (HARD)
If 2A does NOT apply, Opportunity_1 must equal Lowest_Behavior_Category.

Step 3: Rank remaining candidates by fixed priority order
Use this order:
Progression Control
Assumptive Leadership
Accountability Setting (Application)
Accountability Setting (FAFSA)
Accountability Setting (Documents)
Controlled Discovery
Rapport Development
Financial Clarity (Interview)
Process Clarity (FAFSA)
Process Clarity (Documents)
Risk Prevention (Interview)
Risk Prevention (FAFSA)
Technical Guidance Precision (Application)

Step 4: Select outputs
- Opportunity_1 = highest-ranked remaining candidate not already used (unless Step 2A forced it).
- Opportunity_2 = next highest-ranked remaining candidate not already used.
- Opportunity_3 = next highest-ranked remaining candidate not already used.

Step 5: Fill rules
If fewer than three candidates exist, fill remaining Opportunity fields with None.
